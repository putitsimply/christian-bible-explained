# Glossary (helpful words)

Simple meanings for common words in this guide.

* **Apocalyptic writing**: Symbol-filled visions about evil, justice, and hope.
* **Apostle**: “Sent one”; early Christian messenger or leader.
* **Ascension**: Jesus being taken up to heaven (Christian belief).
* **Atonement**: Making things right after wrongdoing; linked to forgiveness.
* **Baptism**: Water ritual showing joining Christianity and a new start.
* **Bible**: Christian sacred writings: stories, poems, laws, letters, visions.
* **Church**: A community of Christians; not just a building.
* **Covenant**: A serious agreement or promise relationship.
* **Crucifixion**: Execution on a cross in the Roman Empire.
* **Disciple**: A close student or follower of a teacher.
* **Epistle**: A letter (many New Testament books are letters).
* **Exile**: Being forced to live away from one’s homeland.
* **Faith**: Trust; in Christianity, trusting God and Jesus.
* **Gentile**: A non-Jewish person.
* **God**: The main divine being worshipped in Judaism and Christianity.
* **Gospel**: “Good news”; also one of the four Jesus biographies.
* **Grace**: God’s kindness as a gift, not earned (Christian idea).
* **Holy Spirit**: God’s active presence (Christian belief).
* **Idol / idolatry**: Treating an object or false god as most important.
* **Israel / Israelites**: Israel is the ancient people/land in the Old Testament; Israelites are the people.
* **Jerusalem**: An important city in the Bible, especially in Judah/Israel.
* **Judah**: The southern kingdom in the Old Testament, with Jerusalem as its capital.
* **LORD**: English Bible way of writing God’s Hebrew name (all caps).
* **Messiah**: “Anointed one”; promised leader. Christians apply it to Jesus.
* **Parable**: A short story used to teach a point.
* **Passover**: Jewish festival remembering escape from slavery in Egypt.
* **Pentecost**: Jewish festival; Acts links it to the Holy Spirit.
* **Pharisees**: A Jewish religious group in Jesus’ time, linked with law and tradition.
* **Priest / high priest**: Temple worship leaders; high priest is the chief.
* **Prayer**: Speaking to God: thanks, praise, questions, requests.
* **Prophet**: Person who speaks messages they believe come from God.
* **Resurrection**: Life after death; Christians believe Jesus rose.
* **Sabbath**: Weekly rest day in Judaism; also in some Christianity.
* **Sacrifice**: Offering in worship; often animals in the Old Testament.
* **Salvation**: Rescue; in Christianity, being saved from sin.
* **Sin**: Wrong actions; also a broken relationship with God (Christian idea).
* **Synagogue**: Jewish meeting place for prayer and teaching.
* **Temple / tabernacle**: Israelite worship places; tabernacle portable, temple permanent.
* **Torah**: First five Bible books; also means “instruction” or “law”.
* **Worship**: Actions showing devotion to God (prayer, songs, rituals).
