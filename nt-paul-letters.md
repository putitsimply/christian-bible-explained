# Letters (Paul)

Many New Testament books are letters sent to real communities and leaders. These letters explain beliefs, answer questions, and give guidance about everyday life.

These are the letters traditionally linked to Paul, an important early Christian teacher and traveller.

## Books in this section

* [Romans](romans.md): a big-picture explanation of Paul’s message and its implications.
* [1 Corinthians](1-corinthians.md): dealing with conflict and practical problems in a church[^gl_church].
* [2 Corinthians](2-corinthians.md): defending Paul’s role and encouraging generosity and resilience.
* [Galatians](galatians.md): arguing about rules, identity, and freedom.
* [Ephesians](ephesians.md): unity, belonging, and living out faith[^gl_faith] in relationships.
* [Philippians](philippians.md): encouragement, joy, and resilience during hardship.
* [Colossians](colossians.md): correcting teachings and focusing on Jesus’ importance.
* [1 Thessalonians](1-thessalonians.md): encouragement and hope about the future.
* [2 Thessalonians](2-thessalonians.md): clarifying confusion and urging steady living.
* [1 Timothy](1-timothy.md): guidance for church leadership and teaching.
* [2 Timothy](2-timothy.md): a personal, urgent letter about staying faithful under pressure.
* [Titus](titus.md): organising communities and encouraging good conduct.
* [Philemon](philemon.md): a short personal letter about forgiveness and relationships.

[^gl_church]: A community of Christians, not just a building. ([Glossary](glossary.md#church))

[^gl_faith]: Trust; in Christianity, trusting God and Jesus. ([Glossary](glossary.md#faith))
